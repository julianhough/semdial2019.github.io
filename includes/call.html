SemDial 2019 - LondonLogue

THE

23rd WORKSHOP ON THE SEMANTICS AND PRAGMATICS OF DIALOGUE

4-6 September, Queen Mary University of London
London

LondonLogue will be the 23rd edition of the SemDial workshop series which aims to bring together researchers working on the semantics and pragmatics of dialogue in fields such as formal semantics and pragmatics, computational linguistics, artificial intelligence, philosophy, psychology, and neuroscience. In 2019 the workshop will be hosted by Queen Mary University of London by the Cognitive Science Group (CogSci), and Computational Linguistics Labs.

WEBSITE: https://semdial2019.github.io

INVITED SPEAKERS:

  • Staffan Larsson (University of Gothenburg)
  • Rosemarie McCabe (City, University of London)
  • Sophie Scott (University College London)

SCOPE:

We invite papers on all topics related to the semantics and pragmatics of dialogue, including, but not limited to:

  • the dynamics of agents' information states in dialogue
  • common ground/mutual belief
  • goals, intentions and commitments in communication
  • turn-taking and interaction control
  • semantic/pragmatic interpretation in dialogue
  • dialogue and discourse structure
  • categorisation of dialogue phenomena in corpora
  • child-adult interaction
  • language learning through dialogue
  • gesture, gaze, and intonational meaning in communication
  • multimodal dialogue
  • interpretation and reasoning in spoken dialogue systems
  • dialogue management
  • designing and evaluating dialogue systems
  • modelling miscommunication, disfluency and repair

SUBMISSIONS:

Authors should submit an anonymous paper of at most 8 pages of content (up to 2 additional pages are allowed for references).

FORMATTING:

Formatting instructions and the URL of the submission site are available on the LondonLogue website.
